Lumpia Recipe

Lumpia

Description

Lumpia is another classic staple of the Philippines and is also recognized as a popular Filipino dish that many foreignors may already feel familiar with.

Similar to an egg roll, it is a fried spring roll traditionally made with a thin pastry skin (a.k.a lumpia wrapper). Upon taking your first bite, you'll be met with a savory mix of either chicken or ground pork and vegetables.

Lumpia can be enjoyed as it is, however it's best paired with either a sweet and sour sauce or vinegar sauce!

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • ½ cup chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • ½ cup minced carrots
  • ½ cup chopped green onions
  • ½ cup thinly sliced green cabbage
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (Optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 30 lumpia wrappers
  • 2 cups vegetable oil for frying, or as needed

Steps

  1. Heat the tablespoon of oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat then add pork
  2. Cook the pork until crumbly with no signs of pink (approximately 5-8 minutes), then remove pork to the side and drain the grease from the pan
  3. Next, sautee onion and garlic to the pan for about 2 minutes. Once fragrant, add in the pork, along with the carrots, green onions, cabbage, and cilantro.
  4. Season with pepper, salt, garlic powder, and soy sauce. Then remove from the heat to let it cool.
  5. Now, assemble the lumpia. Take about 3 heaping tablespoons of filling diagnolly near one corner of a lumpia wrapper, leaving a 1 1/2 inch of space at both ends.
  6. Fold the side along the length of the filling over the filling, tuck in both ends, and roll neatly and tightly to close.
  7. Moisten the other side of the wrapper with water to seal the edge. Transfer to a plate and cover with plastic wrap to retain mositure. Repeat for the remaining.
  8. Now to fry. First, heat 1/2 inch vegetable oil in a heavy skillet over medium heat for 5 minutes.
  9. With the seams face down, slide 3 or 4 lumpias into the hot oil.
  10. While frying for 1-2 minutes, turn occasionally, until all sides are golden brown.
  11. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain and repeat the process for the remaining lumpia.
  12. Lastly, serve while hot either by itself or with a well-paired sauce and enjoy!
