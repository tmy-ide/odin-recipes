Adobo Chicken

Description

Adobo Chicken is considered as the national dish of the Philippines and arguably one of, if not, the most popularly well-known Filipino dish!

Chicken Adobo is known to be sweet yet savory (a very common Filipino flavor style!) with notes of heat from the heavy use of garlic and peppercorn.

Every Filipino household, whether it be in the states or the motherland, has their own varation of how they make the classic and relatively simple Adobo Chicken. So while I will be sharing my own style, anyone reading this can and should experiment with the recipe to satisfy your own palette (as long as you retain the basic Adobo-esque flavor of course!)

Below is the recipe as follows:

Ingredients

  • boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • soy sauce
  • white vinegar
  • onion
  • garlic
  • peppercorns
  • sugar
  • bay leaves
  • green onion

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken briefly (at least one hour)
  2. Once the pan is hot, sear it on the pan
  3. Remove the chicken from the pan and sautee the garlic and onions
  4. Now place the chicken back on the pan and let it simmer with the marinade for 25 minutes until the sauce has reduced
  5. Once the sauce has reduced it should have the consistency of a thick glaze
  6. Now remove the chicken to plate and take the glaze sauce left on the pan and cover it all over the chicken
  7. Lastly, serve with rice (preferrably garlic rice!) and enjoy!
