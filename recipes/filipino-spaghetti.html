Filipino Spaghetti

Description

The Filipino Spaghetti is a funky take on the ever classic Italian spaghetti. While traditional spaghetti is tangy and savory, Filipino spaghetti is known to be sweet. This dish is simple and also something that every kid can enjoy!

Some recipes use sugar as the main sweetener, however, the most traditional way to sweeten the sauce is by using Jufran sauce-- otherwise known as banana ketchip. Gound beef or pork is also mixed into the sauce as well as pork sausage and even hotdog. Upon serving, cheddar cheese is springkled right on top!

Although, technically, not a Filipino dish, its recipe strays from the original which exemplifies the countless other Filipino dishes that are influenced by other cultures.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti noodles
  • 3 tablespoon olive oil ,divided
  • 4 hotdogs ,sliced diagonally
  • 1 onion ,chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic ,minced
  • 1 lb lean ground pork
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 1/2 cups tomato sauce
  • Â½ cup tomato paste
  • 1 cup store-bought banana sauce (banana ketchup)
  • Grated cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti noodles to al dente. Once cooked, drain and toss 1 tablespoon of oil to prevent sticking
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of oil to the pan at medium high heat
  3. (Optional) Hot dogs are already cooked, but if preferred, add hot dogs to pan untl brown and then drain on plate with paper towel.
  4. Add onion and garlic to sautee
  5. Once fragrant and sauteed, add the pork and cook until the color is light brown, juice has evaporated and there is no pink.
  6. Season the pork with salt and pepper to taste
  7. While pork still in pan, add tomato sauce, tomato paste and banana sauce. Stir to combine.
  8. Once fully mixed, reduce heat to medium, cover and let simmer for 15 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  9. A few minutes before removing from heat, add hotdogs and stir.
  10. Serve sauce over spaghetti and springle with grated cheese
